Bug Bounty & Hunter Workshop is a 2-day intensive training course designed to teach you about Bug Bounty programs and how to effectively and efficiently use each program and platform.

Getting started in bug bounty hunting has never been easier, it can be a rewarding, educational or could lead to a new career.

There is no Gartner, Forrester or IDC Magic Quadrant for Bug Bounties, if there was it would encompus Crowd Sourced Security Testing, or Application Crowd Testing Services. However since there is no such thing, we hope our list provides you with information to act on. Below are the top VDP platforms in order of popularity and influence on the bug bounty industry, as well as the top 20 programs and who hosts them, and then each of the platforms customers.

  1. HackerOne – Since 2012, 1700 customers, full feature. Public Bug Bounty Program List: HackerOne
  2. BugCrowd – Since 2011, full feature. Public Bug Bounty Program List Bugcrowd
  3. Open Bug Bounty – 1M coordinated disclosures, 1800 programs
  4. SynAck – Pentesting, AppSec, Bug Bounty, API Testing
  5. Intigriti – Since 2016, Easy to use, private and public bounties
  6. HackenProof – Web3 Bug Bounty Platform, 25k Hackers,
  7. Immunefi – Web3 Bug Bounty Platform
  8. Disclose.io – easy to manage VDP, obtain assistance
  9. Testbirds – Quality Assurance Bug Bounty Program Management
  10. Bugbounter – Mitigate your Risks, Bug Bounty, Red Team, Pentest, VDP
  11. Nordic Defender – More of a (PTaaS) Crowd Sourced Pentesting company
  12. HackRate – Crowdsourced approach for continuous security testing
  13. Bountysource– Segment your projects into self-contained task bug bounties
  14. bugV – Crowdsourcing Cybersecurity Platform, Next Gen Pentesting
  15. Zerocopter – Organize and help you with your VDP
  16. HackTrophy – They put companies in touch with ethical hackers, subscriptions.
  17. BugBounty.JP – Bug Bounty Japan powered by Sprout
  18. YesWeHack – VDP and PTM programs
